Welcome to the interactive visualization of a dataset of photos from flickr. This visualization let's you pick photographs from their geographic location. Then a common ground between these photo's is shown in a graph that is drawn after making your choices. This graph shows how through the labels the common categories are depicted or how through the categories the common labels are depicted. Underneath are a couple of examples on why and how this is usefull and what this visualization can bring you.

  1. If for example, you are a social media influencer and wants to increase your views and social reach. To do so, you want to explore possibilities of adding more hashtags. These hashtags and categories can be searched in our visualization. By searching two differents photos with similar characteristics you can oversee the common tags or categories and use this for his new hashtags or as inspiration for your posts.   2. Or, you are a flickr user and want to add categories or tags to your photos but being new to the photography field you have no clue how to properly describe your photo. With the sunburst filter option you can select categories where you think your photo belongs to, search the map for similar photos and get inspiration for categories/labels/tags.
  3. Or, as an experencied user, you want to search for hotspots for new photo locations. Searching your favorite category or tag, you can compare two hotspots and the amount of views of those hotspots. The statistics of the geography show the comparison between the hotspots. The labels and tags show you if the photos are describing your favorite subjects.

Underneath is shown what the function of every part of the visualization is, the numbers in the list are corresPonding to the numbers in the image on the left.

  1. World map displaying flickr photos and their geographic locations. Locations with a dense grid of photos are aggregated. When hovering/clicking on a dot, the photo with the most views is shown.
  2. Sunburst visualization to filter the world map photos. The first layer is the categories and the second layer the tags for the chosen category.
  3. Photo 1, shown as a green dot on the map, which is the exact location taken from the aggregated photos. The descriptions of the photos are shown underneath it
  4. Photo 2, shown as a blue dot on the map, which is the exact location taken from the aggregated photos. The descriptions of the photos are shown underneath it
  5. Sankey diagram showing the relationship between the two clicked photos. Dependent from what the user is looking for, the user can either look for the relationship between the photo's by looking at the categories or labels.
